FLUX Dance Project

Our goal is to create an open space where Northwestern students can dance for dancing’s sake. We hold weekly dance classes (emphasizing conditioning, improvisation, and new choreography), and provide members with opportunities to participate in all aspects of producing dance concept videos throughout the year.

Project SOAR

Project SOAR matches one Northwestern student with one Evanston child so that the two can have a mutually enriching experience. As a Northwestern mentor, you will meet with your mentee on a weekly basis. Meetings can be throughout Evanston or Chicago and are planned based on your own schedule. SOAR is not a tutoring program, it is meant to provide role models for those children that want and need them.

Wild Roots

Wild Roots Garden was founded at Northwestern University in the fall of 2009 through a collaboration between the One Book One Northwestern project, three undergraduate environmental student organizations (SEED, ECO, and ESW), Facilities Management, the graduate student garden, and Norris University Center.
